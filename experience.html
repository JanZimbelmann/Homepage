As part of a small but highly productive AI team at Appsfactory, my work was focused on backend development - taking projects from scratch and seeing them through to completion largely independently. Each project ran for some months, demanding a high level of personal responsibility and ownership. A significant part of my work involved natural language processing, at a time before large language models like ChatGPT were available - back when AI still required actual engineering rather than a well-worded prompt. The majority of my work involved Python-based development with deployments to Microsoft Azure, using Docker for containerization. Across all projects, I collaborated with other departments using agile methodologies. The clients showcased here are among the most recognized and influential names in German media - and I consider myself fortunate to have worked with them at the cutting edge of AI implementation.

Tagesschau

Tagesschau is Germany's leading public news outlet. For their app, I developed the recommendation system from the ground up - crawling and aggregating data from multiple APIs to build an algorithm in Python that links each article to a set of relevant related articles, updated continuously as new content is published throughout the day. To make this accessible for the frontend team, I designed and deployed a dedicated API on Microsoft Azure, providing a clean and reliable interface that enabled a smooth integration into the final app.

Sportschau

Sportschau is the leading public sports broadcast in Germany. This was my largest project at Appsfactory - and the largest the AI team had taken on at the time. Assigned as my first task, I worked largely autonomously, using their API covering every past and upcoming football match to develop a Python algorithm that automatically generates full game summaries, alongside backend support for the frontend integration. The project was an internal research initiative driven by Sportschau's interest in exploring AI in modern sports journalism.

Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung

The Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung (FAZ), one of Germany's most prestigious daily newspapers, contracted Appsfactory to accelerate their database migration process. Working daily alongside a small team of FAZ data engineers, we defined tasks together and drove the migration forward. My work involved PySpark and SQL in a Databricks-hosted Jupyter Notebook environment, collaborating closely with their in-house data engineering team.

Glamour

Glamour is one of Germany's most well-known lifestyle and fashion magazines, largely recognized for their heavily marketed shopping weeks which attract millions of users in a short period of time. For their app, I implemented a backend recommendation system for the articles available within the app - the same core algorithm developed for Tagesschau, adapted and deployed to fit the specific needs and content structure of the Glamour platform.

< Hackathon />

In April 2023 I participated in the Thin[gk]athon, a hackathon organized by the Smart Systems Hub in Dresden. Commissioned by the Saxon State Chancellery, our challenge was to develop an AI-powered tool to support the IT service desk of the Saxon public sector. Our team from Appsfactory had four days to design and build a functional prototype from scratch - with the challenge handed to us on arrival. We secured 2nd place and 10,000 euros, narrowly beaten by a team whose lead was writing his dissertation on the exact subject - bringing a depth of domain expertise that was genuinely hard to compete with, and well deserving of the win. We couldn't have been happier with 2nd place.

Research Assistant

Alongside my studies, I worked as a research assistant at two institutions - the University of Leipzig and the Helmholtz Centre for Environmental Research (UFZ). Both positions gave me early hands-on experience with scientific computing, data analysis, and working within established research environments, laying the practical foundation for my later work in AI and engineering.

University of Leipzig

At the University of Leipzig, I worked as a research assistant supporting the preparation of the Experimental Physics 2 course (EP 2 - Thermodynamics and Electrodynamics), held by Prof. Dr. Jan Berend Meijer. My work involved preparing course materials for one of the core physics lectures of the undergraduate curriculum.

Helmholtz Centre for Environmental Research

At the UFZ, I designed and built an experimental coaxial measurement cell to measure and reconstruct dielectric properties across a broadband frequency range, combining electronics assembly with computational modeling in Python. The assembly went on to be used by subsequent students for their own Bachelor theses.
